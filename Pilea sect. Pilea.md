## Description
Herbs. Leaves pinnately veined. Inflorescences capitate or subcapitate. male perianth lobes 4, valvate. Female perianth lobes 3, unequal.
  "Statistics": "About ten species: Central and South America, Asia; three species (one introduced) in China.
  "keylist": "
1 Leaf blade obovate or spatulate, 2-7 mm, margin entire.[*P*. *microphylla* 小叶冷水花](Pilea microphylla.md)
1 Leaf blade elliptic-lanceolate or linear, 1.5-9 cm, margin serrate, sometimes bluntly so.——(2)
2(1) Stolon nodes often thickened into conic tubers; leaves crowded on upper 2 or 3 nodes, membranous, linear, 3-9 × 0.5-1 cm, lateral veins 6-10 each side.[*P*. *ternifolia* 羽脉冷水花](Pilea ternifolia.md)
2 Stolon nodes not thickened; leaves on upper stem and branches, succulent, thick papery when dry, elliptic-lanceolate, 1.5-4 × 0.5-1 cm, lateral veins 4-6 each side.[*P*. *penninervis* 钝齿冷水花",](Pilea penninervis.md)
