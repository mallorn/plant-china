龙津蕨",
1.**Mesopteris tonkinensis** (C. Christensen) Ching Acta Phytotax. Sin. 16(4): 22. 1978.",

## Description
Plants to 2 m tall. Stipes angular, to 1.2 m; laminae large, pinnate-pinnatifid, narrowly elliptic, 50-80 × ca. 40 cm; lateral pinnae to 30 pairs, 30-40 × ca. 2 cm, pinnatifid, proximal ones subopposite, distal ones alternate, obliquely spreading, abaxially ± with minute golden, spherical glands; veinlets 8-10 pairs per segment. Sori orbicular, in 1 or more lines along each side of costae, usually arranged in 2-5 irregular lines, not close to costules, or attached on proximal pair of veinlets and close to costules; sterile on distal veinlets.

> On wet rocks in open forests on limestone mountains; ca. 100 m. SW Guangxi (Longjin) [N Vietnam].
  "Synonym": "*Dryopteris tonkinensis* C. Christensen, Bull. Mus. Natl. Hist. Nat., sér. 2, 6: 102. 1934; *Amphineuron tonkinense* (C. Christensen) Holttum; *Cyclosorus tonkinensis* (C. Christensen) L. J. He &amp; X. C. Zhang; *Thelypteris tonkinensis* (C. Christensen) Ching.
