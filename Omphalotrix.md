> One species: China, Korea, Russia (Far East).&#x0D;\n&lt;p&gt;&#x0D;\n&lt;I&gt;Pseudobartsia&lt;/I&gt; is one of the most primitive members of the tribe &lt;I&gt;Rhinantheae&lt;/I&gt;. The genus is related to &lt;I&gt;Odontites&lt;/I&gt; Ludwig and &lt;I&gt;Bartsia&lt;/I&gt; Linnaeus, and it is also similar to\n&lt;I&gt;Phtheirospermum&lt;/I&gt; Bunge. Tao (Acta Bot. Yunnan. 15: 232. 1993) treated &lt;I&gt;Pseudobartsia yunnanensis&lt;/I&gt; as conspecific with the Himalayan &lt;I&gt;Phtheirospermum glandulosum&lt;/I&gt; Bentham.\nHowever, more comparative material is needed before the generic placement of &lt;I&gt;P. glandulosum&lt;/I&gt; and the possible synonymy of &lt;I&gt;P. yunnanensis&lt;/I&gt; can be confirmed.

## Description
Herbs, annual. Leaves opposite. Racemes in panicles. Pedicel elongated. Calyx tube tubular-campanulate, parted medianly to 2/5 length, parted on lateral sides to 1/3-1/2 length, 5-veined. Corolla lower lip deeply 3-lobed, lobes patent; upper lip galeate, straight, margin flat, emarginate. Stamens 4, enclosed by galea; anthers arrow-shaped, locule base extending into spurs, barbate along slit after dehiscence. Stigma capitate. Capsule loculicidal. Seeds numerous; wings white, cross striate.
