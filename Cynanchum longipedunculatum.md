短柱豹药藤",
8.**Cynanchum longipedunculatum** M. G. Gilbert & P. T. Li",

## Description
Stems twining, internodes puberulent along 1 side. Petiole ca. 2.5 cm, slender; leaf blade lanceolate, ca. 5.6 &amp;#215; 2.3 cm, adaxially puberulent, abaxially hairy along veins, base deeply cordate, lobes often overlapping, apex long acuminate; basal veins usually 7, lateral veins 3 or 4 pairs. Inflorescences umbel-like, dense, to 2 cm wide; peduncle 6-9 cm, densely hairy on 1 side, sometimes an isolated flower below main inflorescence. Pedicel 3.5-6 mm, puberulent on 1 side. Sepals oblong lanceolate, ca. 2 &amp;#215; 0.9 mm, puberulent. Corolla white, glabrous outside, densely pilose inside, tube ca. 0.5 mm; lobes ca. 5.5 &amp;#215; 1.7 mm, &amp;#177; erect. Corona cupular, longer than stigma head, slightly fleshy, tube longer than triangular marginal lobes, interior with 5 internal appendages nearly as long as marginal lobes. Anther appendages ovate. Stigma head rounded.

> ca. 3600 m. W China (Sichuan or Hubei)
