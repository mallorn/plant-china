## Description
Lamina bipinnate to tripinnatifid. Pinnae asymmetrical at base. Basiscopic pinnules longer than acroscopic ones. Segments sharply serrate at apex.
  "Statistics": "About five species: Europe and Sino-Himalayan region; three species (one endemic) in China.
