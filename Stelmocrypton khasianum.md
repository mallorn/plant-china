> All parts are used medicinally for bronchitis, cough, and epidemic influenza. The roots yield an aromatic oil.&#x0D;\n&lt;p&gt;&#x0D;\nThe basionym is sometimes given as Periploca khasiana Bentham, but this name was never validated.

## Description
Lianas to 10 m. Branchlets lenticellate, pubescent when young. Petiole ca. 5 mm; leaf blade reddish, oblong-elliptic to elliptic-ovate, 7-17 &amp;#215; 2.5-8 cm, somewhat leathery, glabrous, base cuneate, apex acute or short acuminate; lateral veins ca. 7 pairs, tertiary veins finely reticulate. Inflorescences shorter than leaves, 4- or 5-flowered; peduncle ca. 5 mm. Pedicel ca. 2 mm. Sepals broadly ovate, glabrous. Corolla yellow-green, ca. 6 mm wide, pubescent; lobes ovate. Corona lobes ovate. Follicles oblong-elliptic, 5-9 &amp;#215; ca. 2 cm, glabrous. Seeds ovate; coma ca. 3 cm. Fl. May-Sep, fr. Oct-Dec.

> Bushland, forests. Guangxi, Guizhou, Yunnan [India, Myanmar]
