> &lt;I&gt; Lilium hansonii&lt;/I&gt; is grown as an ornamental.

## Description
Bulb ovoid-globose or subglobose, 3--7 cm in diam.; scales white to yellowish white, ovate to subdeltoid, not articulate. Stem 1--1.5 m. Leaves 4--12-whorled plus a few scattered between apical whorl and basal bract, oblong-oblanceolate, 10--18 × 2--4 cm, apex acuminate. Flowers 4--12 in a raceme, nodding, fragrant. Tepals revolute, yellow or red-orange, with brown spots, lanceolate or oblanceolate, 3--4 cm × 1--1.5 cm, very thick, apex thickened and papillose; nectaries not papillose. Stamens shorter than tepals; filaments glabrous; anthers purplish. Ovary 1--2 cm. Capsule subglobose, 2.5--3.5 cm in diam., 6-winged. Fl. and fr. Jul--Aug. 2&lt;I&gt; n&lt;/I&gt; = 24.

> Possibly naturalized on river banks. S Jilin [native to Korea (Ullung Island)].
