溪荪 (原变种)
7a.**Iris sanguinea var. sanguinea**

> White-flowered forms, which occur in NE Heilongjiang and Japan, have been recognized as &lt;I&gt; Iris sanguinea&lt;/I&gt; f. &lt;I&gt; albiflora&lt;/I&gt; Makino (J. Jap. Bot. 6(11): 32. 1930).


## Description
Inner surface of leaf sheaths without silver-white, metallic gloss. Flowers bluish violet or white, 6--7 cm in diam. Capsule broadly cylindric, long, 3.5--5 × 1.2--1.5 cm, glabrous. 2&lt;I&gt; n&lt;/I&gt; = 28*.


> Damp meadows, sunny pond banks, hillsides; ca. 500 m. Heilongjiang, Jilin, Liaoning, Nei Mongol [Japan, Korea, Mongolia, Russia].

  "Synonym": "&lt;I&gt; Iris extremorientalis&lt;/I&gt; Koidzumi; &lt;I&gt; I. nertschinskia&lt;/I&gt; Loddiges; &lt;I&gt; I. sibirica&lt;/I&gt; Linnaeus var. &lt;I&gt; orientalis&lt;/I&gt; Baker; &lt;I&gt; I. sibirica&lt;/I&gt; var. &lt;I&gt; sanguinea&lt;/I&gt; (Donn ex Hornemann) Ker Gawler.
