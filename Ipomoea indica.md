8.**Ipomoea indica** (J. Burman) Merrill",

> Fosberg (Bot. Not. 129: 35-38. 1976) has sorted out the complicated nomenclature for this pantropical species and established that &lt;I&gt;Ipomoea&lt;/I&gt; &lt;I&gt;indica&lt;/I&gt; is the correct name for it.

## Description
Herbs twining or sometimes prostrate, with &amp;#177; densely retrorse pilose axial parts. Stems 3-6 m, sometimes rooting at nodes. Petiole 2-18 cm; leaf blade ovate or circular, 5-15 X 3.5-14 cm, abaxially densely short, soft, pubescent, adaxially &amp;#177; sparsely pubescent, base cordate, margin entire or &amp;#177; 3-lobed, apex acuminate or abruptly acuminate. Inflorescences dense umbellate cymes, several flowered; peduncle 4-20 cm; bracts linear, sometimes lanceolate. Pedicel 2-5(-8) mm. Sepals subequal, 1.4-2.2 cm, gradually linear-acuminate apically, glabrous to appressed pilose; outer 3 lanceolate to broadly lanceolate; inner 2 narrowly lanceolate. Corolla bright blue or bluish purple, aging reddish purple or red, with a paler center, funnelform, 5-8 cm, glabrous. Stamens included. Pistil included; ovary glabrous. Stigma 3-lobed. Capsule &amp;#177; globose, 1-1.3 cm in diam. Seeds ca. 5 mm. 2&lt;I&gt;n&lt;/I&gt; = 30*.
