> Subfamily Dryopteridoideae as defined here is based on morphological features and has not yet been recovered as monophyletic using molecular data.

## Description
Fronds monomorphic, if dimorphic then rachis with proliferous bulbils borne at a prolonged flagelliform rachis terminal (*Polystichum* sect. *Cyrtomiopsis*). Sporangia in orbicular sori.
  "Statistics": "About 17 genera and 1,400 species: nearly cosmopolitan, but highest diversity found in E Asia (*Dryopteris*, *Polystichum*); six genera and 459 species (244 endemic) in China.
