> Chinese records of &lt;I&gt; Fritillaria meleagris&lt;/I&gt; Linnaeus (e.g., in FRPS) are referable to &lt;I&gt; F. meleagroides&lt;/I&gt;.

## Description
Bulb of 2 or 3 scales, subglobose, 0.5--1.5 cm in diam. Stem 20--40 cm. Leaves 3--7, alternate; leaf blade linear, 5--15 cm × 1--5 mm, apex sometimes curved. Inflorescence usually 1-flowered; bract solitary, apex acuminate. Flower nodding, campanulate; pedicel variable in length. Tepals deep purple or dark brownish violet, slightly tessellated or speckled; outer ones oblong-elliptic, 2--3.5 cm × 5--8 mm; inner ones obovate, 2--3.5 × 0.7--1.2 cm; nectaries linear, ca. 15 × 2 mm. Stamens ca. 2/3 as long as tepals; filaments papillose. Style 3-lobed; lobes 4--8 mm. Capsule wingless. Fl. May--Jun. 2&lt;I&gt; n&lt;/I&gt; = 18, 24*.

> Mud flats, wet meadows, swampy fields; 900--2400 m. NW Xinjiang [Kazakhstan, Russia; E Europe].
