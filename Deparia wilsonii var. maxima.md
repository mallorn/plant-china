> The epithet was first introduced as *“Lunathyrium maximum”* (Ching in Y. L. Zhang et al., Sporae Pterid. Sin. 233. 1976), but this was not validly published because no Latin description or diagnosis, or reference to such, was provided (*Melbourne Code*, Art. 39.1).

## Description
Plants large (up to 100 cm or more). Pinnae and pinna segments broader, pinna segments prominently toothed at margin. Hairs on rachis and costae abaxially thin and short.

> ● Mixed forests, wet areas; 2200-3100 m. S Gansu, Sichuan.
