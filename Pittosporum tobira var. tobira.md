海桐(原变种)",
3a.**Pittosporum tobira var. tobira**",

## Description
Young branchlets and leaf blade pubescent. Inflorescences, bracts, and sepals densely brown pubescent. Ovary densely pubescent. Fl. Mar–May, fr. Jun–Oct.

> Cultivated for ornament and possibly naturalized, forests, limestone areas, slopes, sandy seashores, roadsides;; sea level to 1800 m. Fujian, Guangdong, Guangxi, Guizhou, Hainan, Hubei, Jiangsu, Sichuan, Yunnan, Zhejiang [native to S Japan and S Korea (including Cheju Island)].
  "Synonym": "*Euonymus tobira* Thunberg, Nova Acta Regiae Soc. Sci. Upsal. 3: 208. 1780; *Pittosporum tobira* var. *chinense* S. Kobayashi.
